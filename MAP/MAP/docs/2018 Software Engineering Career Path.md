ac:type="single"><ac:layout-cell><h1>Engineering Path - TRAC descriptions</h1></ac:layout-cell></ac:layout-section><ac:layout-section ac:type="three_equal"><ac:layout-cell><ac:structured-macro ac:name="panel" ac:schema-version="1" ac:macro-id="acd0d1af-584d-4624-8585-72e1c510fbfd"><ac:parameter ac:name="title">Software Engineer I</ac:parameter><ac:rich-text-body><p><strong>Software Engineer I are responsible for executing tasks with oversight and within an already existing project.</strong></p><p>For SE I - their skill level is still unknown or it is known and the engineer needs a lot of hand holding (from other engineers) to complete any non-trivial amount of work.</p><p><span>These engineers should be focused on learning team standards, tools, languages, and principles during the development of their tasks. They should often be paired with more senior engineers for mentorship and code reviews to assist with their growth.</span></p><p><strong>Scope and Influence</strong>: Self</p></ac:rich-text-body></ac:structured-macro><p>&nbsp;</p><p>&nbsp;</p></ac:layout-cell><ac:layout-cell><ac:structured-macro ac:name="panel" ac:schema-version="1" ac:macro-id="6bbe5780-71df-4695-8f86-09299a92ac1e"><ac:parameter ac:name="title">Software Engineer II</ac:parameter><ac:rich-text-body><p><strong>Software Engineer II are responsible for executing on tasks within an already existing application following development standards set by technical leads.</strong></p><p><span><span style="color: rgb(73,73,73);">An SE II </span></span><span style="color: rgb(73,73,73);">is an engineer who can work on and complete a small&nbsp;</span><span style="color: rgb(73,73,73);">well-defined</span><span style="color: rgb(73,73,73);">&nbsp;task but doesn't yet have the skills and experience to be a senior engineer.</span></p><p><span style="color: rgb(73,73,73);">Software engineer II's<span>&nbsp;tend not to have a full grasp of the&nbsp;</span>software development life-cycle<span>&nbsp;</span><span>yet. These engineers may also not understand the full architecture of the application or system they work on.</span></span></p><p>Software engineer II's should be participating in the technology community by attending events and starting to contribute back through presentations or technology contributions.</p><p><strong>Scope and Influence</strong>: Team</p></ac:rich-text-body></ac:structured-macro></ac:layout-cell><ac:layout-cell><ac:structured-macro ac:name="panel" ac:schema-version="1" ac:macro-id="75256069-8e80-4e73-81d4-0e54ea24f584"><ac:parameter ac:name="title">Senior Software Engineer</ac:parameter><ac:rich-text-body><p><strong>Senior Software Engineers are responsible for large portions of the development of an application, executing at a high level and degree of quality.</strong></p><p><span style="color: rgb(73,73,73);">A&nbsp;</span>senior engineer<span style="color: rgb(73,73,73);">&nbsp;can take a high level (and often vague) task and break it down into small&nbsp;</span><span style="color: rgb(73,73,73);">well-defined</span><span style="color: rgb(73,73,73);">&nbsp;tasks themselves. These engineers are capable of delivering high quality while effectively minimizing technical debt and impact.</span></p><p><span style="color: rgb(73,73,73);"><span>Senior engineers spend a <strong>non-trivial</strong> amount of time mentoring and <strong>effectively</strong> improving the knowledge and productivity of others in their <strong>team</strong>.</span></span></p><p><span style="color: rgb(73,73,73);"><span>The senior engineer</span><span>&nbsp;</span><span>has good <span>architecture skills such that things like maintainability and testability are addressed</span>. Typically, a senior software engineer can be trusted to work independently to solve most problems and to identify or help fix company-wide concerns such as production support and outages.</span></span></p><p><span><strong>Scope and Influence</strong>: Multiple teams or projects</span></p></ac:rich-text-body></ac:structured-macro></ac:layout-cell></ac:layout-section><ac:layout-section ac:type="two_equal"><ac:layout-cell><ac:structured-macro ac:name="panel" ac:schema-version="1" ac:macro-id="6090fba9-dac2-4a2b-9915-2b161dd6c9a8"><ac:parameter ac:name="titleColor">white</ac:parameter><ac:parameter ac:name="titleBGColor">#D04437</ac:parameter><ac:parameter ac:name="title">Architect</ac:parameter><ac:rich-text-body><p><strong>Architects are responsible for designing and communicating the vision and strategy of an application domain and its dependencies.</strong></p><p>Architects spend a <strong>significant</strong> amount of time mentoring and <strong>effectively</strong> improving the knowledge and productivity of others in their <strong>development group</strong>.</p><p>Architects are passionate about technology and are focused on learning and teaching others. Software Architects should be investing a significant amount of time understanding the needs of the business to plan and design the future state of their systems so that the company is building software for tomorrow while also supporting the needs of today.</p><p><strong>Scope and Influence</strong>: Domain</p></ac:rich-text-body></ac:structured-macro></ac:layout-cell><ac:layout-cell><ac:structured-macro ac:name="panel" ac:schema-version="1" ac:macro-id="7af3c7af-a752-407a-a6cc-01f6f1b89dc4"><ac:parameter ac:name="titleColor">white</ac:parameter><ac:parameter ac:name="titleBGColor">#3B73AF</ac:parameter><ac:parameter ac:name="title">Staff Software Engineer</ac:parameter><ac:rich-text-body><p><strong>Staff Engineers have become experts in their space by setting development standards, pushing them forward, and deliver high quality software.</strong></p><p>Staff engineers are often the engineering lead of significant projects. These engineers have perfected their craft in their chosen toolset or language (for example more than 10,000 hours experience). They can build nearly any type of system with their current toolset and are among the best engineers in the company.</p><p>Staff engineers spend a <strong>non-trivial</strong> amount of time mentoring and <strong>effectively</strong> improving the knowledge and productivity of others in their <strong>development group</strong>.</p><p>Staff engineers are actively involved in: architecture, engineering, and process improvements as well as implementing those changes. They will likely be the primary expert for multiple systems and an effective contact for any issue or troubleshooting for systems in their development group.</p><p><span><strong>Scope and Influence</strong>: Domain</span></p></ac:rich-text-body></ac:structured-macro></ac:layout-cell></ac:layout-section><ac:layout-section ac:type="two_equal"><ac:layout-cell><p>&nbsp;</p></ac:layout-cell><ac:layout-cell><ac:structured-macro ac:name="panel" ac:schema-version="1" ac:macro-id="56135b0b-8a36-4a04-aa1d-5a07d8e06c55"><ac:parameter ac:name="titleColor">white</ac:parameter><ac:parameter ac:name="titleBGColor">#3B73AF</ac:parameter><ac:parameter ac:name="title">Principal Engineer</ac:parameter><ac:rich-text-body><p><strong>Principal Engineers have a proven history of building and designing robust systems at a high level.</strong></p><p>Principal engineers can effectively go deep in multiple development groups and are often the company expert in multiple domains. These engineers can architect, design, and build complete systems while maintaining project deadlines and delivering high quality solutions.</p><p>Principal engineers spend a <strong>non-trivial</strong> amount of time mentoring and <strong>effectively</strong> improving the knowledge and productivity of others in <strong>the company or multiple </strong><strong>development group.</strong></p><p>Principal engineers have the knowledge equivalent to multiple Lead engineers and actively pursue new technologies that can have significant impact on their entire company. These engineers often need no one to set their agenda - as they will have a substantial self-identified backlog. Principal engineers focus on pushing the company technology forward and always remaining <strong>effective</strong> in their role as an engineer.</p><p>Principal engineers are mission critical to several systems and retain vast industry knowledge. Their loss would have a significant impact to the company.</p><p><strong>Scope and Influence</strong>: Multiple domains or Loans.</li></ul><p>&nbsp;</p><p>Note:&nbsp;<strong>Scope</strong>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<strong>Experience</strong>&nbsp;are guidelines only and based on expectations only.
